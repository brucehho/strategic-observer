Three US fighter jets downed. The American myth, shattered.

The situation has reversed completely. The blitzkrieg that the US and Israel planned now looks essentially dead on arrival. Why? Look at the ferocity of Iran's counterattack and you'll understand.

The original US script went something like this: eliminate Khamenei and the top leadership first, paralyze the command structure, follow with a massive air campaign to destroy Iran's missile industry, quietly install a few pro-American figures, and through this combination strike force Iran to capitulate and become the next Venezuela. No one expected the situation to ignore the script entirely.

Just three days into the war, Iran's counteroffensive arrived — and it drew blood with every blow. The core reason: Iran's air defense system was not knocked out. In fact, within a single day it downed three American fighter jets. The US itself confirmed the loss of three F-15s. American airpower has not suffered humiliation on this scale since Vietnam.

Beyond the air war, Iran's ground-based counterattacks left US military hardware badly exposed. In Qatar, a drone strike destroyed a THAAD core radar vehicle — a $1 billion air defense system was rendered blind in an instant. More critically, from Kuwait to the UAE to Saudi Arabia, the layered network of Patriot-3 and THAAD batteries — America's premier air defense architecture — was comprehensively penetrated by Iranian missiles.

The downstream consequences run deep. Arab nations across the Middle East watched helplessly as the expensive American systems they purchased, backed by US troops on the ground, still left their airspace porous as a sieve. After this, who would dare rely on America entirely? When the dust settles, many of them will likely be shopping for Chinese air defense systems as a second layer of insurance.

But there is more bad news for the US-Israel side. Netanyahu's own office was struck by an Iranian missile. He had fled in time — first to Germany, then to Cyprus — before quietly returning. Yet something curious happened during the opening days of the conflict: two Iranian missiles struck Cyprus without apparent explanation. No one could work it out then. Now the reason has been confirmed — they were tracking Netanyahu.

This reveals something critical: Iran's intelligence network was never severed. It is still locked onto the movements of Israeli leadership. Netanyahu and his circle have been placed on a revenge list. And this is where the US-Israel strategy fundamentally miscalculated. They believed that killing Khamenei would throw Iran into chaos and break its will to fight. In reality, Khamenei — while alive — was the one person with whom the United States could actually sit down and negotiate. Now that he is gone, no single figure inside Iran commands enough authority to order the Revolutionary Guards to stand down. The country no longer has room to compromise. All that remains is the path of total resistance.

The US-Israel coalition badly underestimated Iran's resolve. A blitzkrieg has become a grinding war, and the ceasefire key is no longer in their possession. Before the war began, Iranians in Tehran's Revolution Square were still chanting anti-American slogans in the streets. When the first US strikes landed and Washington expected Iran to fold, the missiles that had been gathering dust in warehouses for years were finally called into service. Where this war goes next, everyone is weighing carefully.
