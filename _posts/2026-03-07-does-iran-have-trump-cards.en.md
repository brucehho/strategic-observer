## What trump cards can Iran use to sustain the fight against the US?

Iran's missile offensive over the past few days has caught many off guard. Few expected Iran to hit this hard. That has forced Trump to return home to seek more domestic support. According to US media, the US Central Command has asked the Pentagon to deploy more military intelligence personnel to sustain operations against Iran for at least 100 days, possibly through September. Of course, the US and Israel will not concede easily. The US has ramped up its offensive. Rumors suggest the US is pushing Kurds into northwestern Iran to replace American ground forces and wage a ground war against Iran. For the US, that's a clever move. Besides, although US and Israeli missile interception has depleted reserves rapidly, the US has another fallback: with Iran's air defenses largely gone, the US can rely on conventional bombs for broader bombardment. The US is throwing everything it has.

What about Iran? First, a point that may disappoint some: the US using Kurds to fight Iran on the ground is not good news for the US—it means the US wants to leave. On the surface it's backing the Kurds; in reality it's dumping the mess on them. The day after Khamenei's assassination, we said in our video that the US had bitten off more than it could chew. Some disagree, arguing Iran will be toppled by the US, which will then replicate the same playbook elsewhere—a scenario very bad for China. But I see it differently. **I believe the US overreached by killing Khamenei: it will provoke Iranians to resist and retaliate fiercely against the US.** That view is the foundation for how the war has unfolded these past days and will continue to unfold.

**Iran holds three trump cards, backed by China's normal trade: hypersonic missiles, the resistance axis scattered across the Middle East, and the blockade of the Strait of Hormuz.**

Some are pessimistic: Iran's leader Khamenei is dead, over forty senior officials are dead, Iran must lose. In fact we laid out **Iran's three war strategies**:

First, the US is unwilling and Israel is incapable of a ground war. Iran must hold tight to this—it's the bottom line. At worst, that's where things stand. In other words, as long as the US avoids a ground war, Iran only needs to withstand the bombing to turn the tide and avoid a dictated peace, forced surrender, or regime change.

Second, blockade the Strait of Hormuz.

Third, reorganize and activate the resistance axis to strike from the flanks. The second is defense, the third is offense—together they give Iran both flexibility and leverage.

## Why would Iran attack Saudi refineries?

Some might think it's revenge on Saudi Arabia, to disrupt global energy supply chains and counter US sanctions. Iran has also struck US bases in the region with missiles and drones, angering the UAE, Bahrain, Qatar, and others. Then it blockaded Hormuz, disrupting global energy transit and drawing more condemnation. At first glance, Iran seems to have alienated many countries; some have threatened retaliation. Some conclude Iran has overreached and will hurt itself. But this is necessary—we're not saying Iran is right or wrong, only viewing it from Iran's perspective. **Iran's attacks on Saudi refineries and the Hormuz blockade are deliberate.** Why? To tell Saudi Arabia and its neighbors: you cannot stay neutral. Some countries said they wouldn't open their airspace to US attacks. When the US actually used bases in those countries to strike Iran, they said they had no choice: "We don't want the US to use our bases against Iran, but if the US insists, we can't stop them."

Iran will not tolerate such fence-sitting. If Iran tolerates it, it would be gambling with its own survival. A few missiles in the past were one thing; the US and Israel are now openly trying to destroy Iran and replace its regime.

**Iran is clearly warning Saudi Arabia and its neighbors: do not participate in the US war on Iran; do not fence-sit.** Do not pretend to want peace and sympathize with Iran while doing the opposite. If these countries still fence-sit, Iran will strike. This is Iran's survival. Iran is fighting the world's superpower and the Middle East's military hegemon. Iran cannot afford to look the other way as it did before. The US is using military bases in these countries to its advantage—and pushing them into the fire, drawing them into the conflict. Why? These countries spent huge sums on US arms, including air defense missiles and radar, to protect themselves. Instead, the US is using them to intercept Iranian missiles. They paid the bill, but the US is calling the shots. They will spend more to replenish missiles and facilities. They've been made fools of.

## Why is Iran blocking the Strait of Hormuz?

Many understand it as Iran dragging everyone down—if we sink, we sink together. But it's more than that. **Blocking Hormuz can deter countries from participating in the US military campaign against Iran.** Didn't Britain refuse US use of UK-controlled bases to attack Iran? Especially Diego Garcia in the Indian Ocean—Britain only changed its tune after its base in Cyprus was hit by Iran. France only allows US use of its bases for necessary defense. Since the 2022 Ukraine conflict, European economies have suffered, energy prices have soared, inflation has risen. After Iran's Hormuz blockade, Brent crude has risen from $70 to $85/barrel. If Iran continues, Brent could breach $100. The logic is direct: the US won't let me sell oil, sanctions and blockade. In peacetime I can tolerate it. Now the US has launched a formal attack, a war—I won't tolerate it. Why talk of gentlemanly conduct? If we die, we die together. Iran has even announced that if the US and Israel strike Iran's oil facilities and prevent Iran from producing or refining oil, Iran will strike oil facilities across the Middle East. Everyone goes down together. This is Iran's survival war. As long as the US and Israel don't destroy Iran's oil industry, Iran won't do this—everyone has a way out. Otherwise, everyone goes down.

The US, Europe, and Japan are all affected. Japan is hit first—inflation has risen in recent years, the yen has depreciated, and energy price spikes add fuel to the fire. Japan's PM Takayuki is still pushing QE and military expansion. Now oil prices are high and there's no firewood left. Iran doesn't actually want to blockade Hormuz. For years it has been hit by Israel and the US without doing so. But now it's survival time.

## Why should Iran adopt guerrilla tactics and a war of attrition?

Because the US and Israel cannot sustain a long war. Israel is small, resource-poor, and manpower-limited—it's ill-suited for attrition. The US, though a superpower, is unwilling to be bogged down; it wants to save strength and resources for China. War isn't just about numbers or who has better weapons—it's about how the whole force is deployed. **Iran can fight ground wars—that's Iran's strength, and it matters.** With that, Iran can hold out. The US and Israel won't fight on the ground. Iran has hypersonic missiles; the US and Israel don't. That's Iran's trump card, combined with drones first, missiles in support, hypersonics last. Iran holds Hormuz and the resistance axis disperses US-Israeli forces. These are Iran's aces.

Iran has another ace: Iran is estimated to have tens of thousands of drones—at least tens of thousands. Iran's total drone fleet exceeds that of the US and Israel. The US can only deploy drones from elsewhere. Iran produces hundreds of drones per month in peacetime; in wartime it can ramp up to thousands. The US and Israel claimed they would destroy Iran's missile industry, but that hasn't happened. Iran is conserving missiles for a long war. Iran has already destroyed many US-Israeli assets. No rush—wait for them to replenish before striking again, so as not to waste missiles. Iran is playing guerrilla warfare with the US and Israel, preserving strength. The US-Israel coalition has air superiority—so Iran plays along, emerging when needed to launch missiles. This is real missile guerrilla warfare. Iran's current approach will write a new chapter in military textbooks.

## Does China really not participate?

Beijing has appeared with a mediation posture—to give all sides a way out. Only China has the strength, weight, and credibility to mediate. If Iran ultimately fares badly, would Beijing stay out?

Beijing stays out now because it has calculated that Iran can hold out and thus doesn't need to intervene.

If China mediates and gives the US a way out, would the US refuse? If the US refuses, would it be forcing China to act? That would be refusing a toast only to drink a forfeit. Beijing stays out for two reasons: it aligns with China's traditional non-interference policy, and if the US sinks into another prolonged war, that benefits China and Russia. We said on day one of the war that the US had bitten off more than it could chew by killing Khamenei: it will likely be bogged down in another war. China is in no hurry.

**Iran dares to fight, is willing to fight, and can fight—precisely because China supports it through normal trade.** Normal trade means stable supply chains and, in wartime, the means to sustain military needs. Remember 2022: Russia held on because of China's normal trade. If Iran aligns with China, Iran can find the weapons and parts it needs on the plateau. This US-Israel-Iran war is shaping the world. Iran is at the center. Iran, the US, and Israel—this three-way game is gripping.
